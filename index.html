What's eWaste?

eWaste is a term used to describe any electronic device that is outdated, obsolete, broken, donated, discarded, or at the end of its useful life. This E-waste has been growing at an exponential rate due to the rapid expansion of technology, everyday more and more eWaste is being generated each year.

Trashed Electronics Are Harmful - TEMP TEXT

eWaste in landfills contain dangerous chemicals that can seep into the ground, contaminating groundwater and the soil. These chemicals are extremely harmful both to the environment and to human health.

Batteries Computers Cell Phones TVs
Lithium causes fires that release more chemicals into the air we breath. Nickel and Cadnium are carcinogens. Lead causes birth defects and mercury is highly toxic, especially in vapor form. Computers contain large amounts of Persistent Bioaccumulative and Toxic Chemicals, or PBTs, such as lithium and mercury. When disposed of improperly, these hazardous materials seep into the water supply, which can be dangerous for animals and humans alike. Cell phones use PBTs, which are hazardous substances that cause pollution to the environment and health problems to humans and animals. For example, cell phones contain lead, which cause developemtal disorders, and mercury, which cause sensory impairments, among other things. Televisions contain copious amounts of lead, which harms the central nervous system. If not disposed of properly, the lead can seep into the surrounding area and affect hundreds of people.

Recycled Electronics Give Back - TEMP TEXT

Almost all of the materials used to manufacture electronics can be recovered to make new products. Metals, plastics, and rechargeable batteries are turned into new materials and products.

A number of different metals – gold, silver, platinum, palladium, copper, tin, and zinc - are recycled and used by different industries such as jewelry, plating, electronics, automotive, and art foundries.

The plastics recovered are recycled into plastic components for new electronic devices or other plastic products.
